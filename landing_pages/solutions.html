01

Watertight Integrity

The primary failure mode for river and nearshore cruisers is "progressive flooding" following a collision or grounding event. Traditional designs often treat the hull as a single volume or rely on minimal bulkheads that do not extend above the waterline.

The Open Yacht implements a rigorous 7-Zone Compartmentation Strategy derived from SOLAS principles, ensuring the vessel remains afloat and stable with any single compartment breached.

LEGEND: ― Watertight Bulkhead
Z6 High Risk Zone
--- Design Waterline

Penetration Policy

"Swiss cheese" bulkheads defeat the purpose of compartmentation. We enforce a strict hierarchy for penetrations:

  • 01. Route all cables/pipes overhead (Deckhead) to avoid submarine penetrations.
  • 02. Use certified glands or MCT blocks for all bulkhead transits. No silicone.
  • 03. Steering gear in Z7 (Lazarette) isolated via watertight boots.

Bilge Redundancy

Rather than relying solely on individual electric pumps which can clog or lose power, we employ a Central Manifold System. A high-capacity emergency pump (engine driven or manual) can draw from any zone via a selector manifold located in the cockpit.

02

Composite Grid

The hull is built to ISO 12215 standards using a "hybrid core" strategy. We prioritize impact resistance in the lower sections and stiffness/weight optimization in the topsides.

FIG 2.1: HULL LAMINATE STACKUP
RESIN SYSTEM

Vinylester Skin

The outer 3 layers utilize Vinylester resin for superior osmosis protection and secondary bonding, backed by Isophthalic Polyester for structural bulk.

CORE MATERIAL

Closed Cell Foam

Topsides use PVC foam core to increase panel stiffness without weight. The bottom hull remains Solid GRP to eliminate delamination risk during groundings.

FRAMING

Longitudinal

A system of 4 hat-section stringers per side supports the hull panels, reducing the unsupported span and allowing for a lighter shell.

03

Hybrid Propulsion

To solve the cost/efficiency equation, we utilize a Serial Hybrid EREV (Extended Range Electric Vehicle) architecture. This decouples the engine from the propeller, allowing the diesel generator to run at its peak efficiency RPM regardless of boat speed.

FIG 3.1: EREV DRIVETRAIN SCHEMATIC
POWER SOURCE: DIESEL / BATTERY
TRANSMISSION: BELT REDUCTION (3:1)
Donor Drivetrain Philosophy By repurposing high-volume automotive hybrid components (Motors, Inverters) instead of low-volume marine diesels, we achieve a 60% reduction in propulsion hardware costs. The system uses a belt-reduction drive to match the high-RPM electric motors to the efficient low-RPM propeller curve.
Silent Running The battery buffer allows for 2-4 hours of completely silent cruising at low speeds (5-6 knots). The generator engages automatically only when the battery state-of-charge drops below 30% or when high power is commanded.
04

AC-First Grid

We term our electrical philosophy "The Floating Apartment." Instead of relying on expensive, heavy low-voltage (12/24V) DC cabling for house loads, we run a robust 220V AC Backbone.

FIG 4.1: SINGLE LINE DIAGRAM

Residential Compatibility

By standardizing on 220V, we enable the use of high-volume residential appliances (Induction cooktops, Inverter Fridges, Split Air Conditioners) which are 1/4th the cost of "marine grade" equivalents and often more reliable/efficient.

Safety Layers

Every AC circuit is protected by an RCBO (Residual Current Breaker with Overload). Wet zones (Galley, Head) use high-sensitivity 10mA trips. A full galvanic isolation transformer separates shore ground from hull ground to prevent corrosion.

05

The Build Process

BMAD: Build, Measure, Assess, Design

B

We generate a complete "virtual build" including all bill of materials and cutting lists before a single sheet of plywood is purchased.

M

We measure the cost impact of every design decision using real-time local pricing from our Vietnam supply chain.

A

We assess the trade-offs. Does saving $500 on resin compromise the laminate impact strength? (The answer is always "Don't do it").

D

We refine the design. The result is a construction plan that is optimized for the specific skills and materials available at the yard.
